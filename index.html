our CoSchool

Secondary schools are experiencing a critical and growing shortage of qualified specialist teachers in STEM and specifically ICT. Many schools have capable, dedicated teachers that would deliver these courses if they were appropriately trained.

CoSchool partners with businesses, and government and independent schools to provide high quality, specialist leadership, education and technology services.

HOW WE WORK

We develop close working relationships with organisations and people, built
on trust and integrity. We promote open and honest communication and
build ongoing connections with key leaders and employees.

Initial Discussion

An initial discussion with School Leader(s) to present the program and answer questions.

  • Describe the process, benefits, time commitments, program options, risk management, strategy and costs.
  • Assessment of existing teacher(s) and school capabilities, requirements and constraints for potential fit.
  • Identify specific concerns that could include items such as teacher retention, quality assurance, timing etc.

Holistic Assessment

An information gathering workshop to capture all relevant information required to develop the uplift program.

  • Participants: Teacher(s) and, optionally, School Leaders
  • Duration: 1x4 hour or 2x2 hour workshops
  • Focus areas:
    • Knowledge across relevant technology domains
    • Breadth of teaching experience
    • Program development experience
    • Specific school custom and practice
    • Constraints such as timeline and teacher workload

Uplift program discussion

An in-depth discussion of the program structure, content and recommendations.

  • Participants: School leaders and teacher(s)
  • Duration: 1x4 hour or 2x2 hour sessions
  • Program content:
    • Teacher learning material and strategy
    • Sample teaching programs and assessments
  • Timeline
  • Next Steps:
    • The school can choose to deliver the program with the support of CoSchool or independently.

CoSchool Guided Implementation

CoSchool supports your teachers as they learn and develop and transition into teaching the new courses. This includes:

  • 1 or 2-term duration through a flexible block hours model
  • Subject matter expertise and regular mentoring and check-ins
  • Advice on teaching programs, assessments, and staff development
